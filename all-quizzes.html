Case Study 2: Tammy

Tammy is a 25 year old woman, gravida 3, para 2002 at 37 5/7 weeks gestation. Two previous pregnancies resulted in spontaneous vaginal births at 38 6/7 weeks gestation and 39 4/7 weeks gestation. She is obese (pre-pregnancy BMI 36) with no other relevant medical history. Surgical history includes an appendectomy at age 10. The father of this baby is different than the father of Tammy's other two children, and he is curently deployed overseas. Tammy's current obstetric history: weight gain of 56 lbs with this pregnancy; mild preeclampsia; 2nd consistent with the last menstrual period (LMP) and normal anatomy; and 3rd trimester ultrasounds show normal growth.

Case Study 3: April

April is a 28-year-old woman, G1P0 who is 40 1/7 weeks gestation by LMP and first trimester ultrasound examination. She came to the labor unit complaining of irregular contractions. She denies vaginal discharge or leaking of fluid. She states her fetus is as active as usual and she has been performing fetal kick counts after meals since the prenatal visit with her primary care physician last week. She hopes she will be admitted because yesterday was her due date and she is tired of being pregnant.

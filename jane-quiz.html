Case Study: Jane

Jane is a 30 year old, married, gravida 1 para 0 at 41 3/7 weeks. She has no relevant medical history and no past surgeries. Current Obstetric History: Ultrasound. Nonstress test at 40 1/7 weeks was reactive after 40 minutes. Jane missed a nonstress test two days ago due to her car breaking down and no other options for transportation. Induction scheduled.

On admission, Jane tells her nurse that she has not felt her baby move today, and she is concerned because her baby usually moves frequently and noticeably. The nurse applies the external EFM components and the tracing from 0932 represents Jane's initial tracing.

Vaginal examination reveals Jane is 2 cm/50%/-1 station, cephalic presentation and her membranes ruptured spontaneously during the vaginal examination with thick meconium present in amniotic fluid, moderate amount and no vaginal bleeding. Jane's nurse performed fetal scalp stimulation during the examination, but there was no FHR acceleration in response. Jane's vital signs: 120/78, temp 97.4, pulse 84, respirations 20.
